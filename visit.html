Cridersville Local History Museum

Address: 111 W. Sugar Street, Cridersville, OH 45806

Open: Second and Fourth Sunday of each month (April–October), 1:00–4:00 p.m.

Parking: Street parking and behind-museum parking

The Cridersville Local History Museum has grown into a space filled with stories, exhibits, and educational experiences that are actively shared with the community. The museum features themed exhibit areas that guide visitors through Cridersville’s history, and it regularly welcomes visitors of all ages—including school tours and youth groups—supported by trained volunteer guides.

Tip: For group visits or school tours, please contact us in advance so we can schedule guides.

Bowsher Log Home

Location: East Main Street, Cridersville, OH

Parking: Parking at Cridersville Health Care Center, 603 E. Main Street

The Bowsher Log Home is a restored 19th-century log home that reflects the early settlement and craftsmanship of the Cridersville area. Donated by the Bowsher family, the cabin serves as a historic landmark and educational space, offering a glimpse into daily life during the village’s early years.

What to Expect

  • Friendly volunteer guides (training and scripts provided).
  • Family-friendly exhibits and community stories.
  • Seasonal open houses and special events.

Accessibility varies by building and exhibit area. Please contact us with specific needs and we’ll do our best to accommodate.
