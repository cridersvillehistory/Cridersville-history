Welcome

Welcome to the Cridersville Historical Society, where local history comes to life through community, tradition, and shared experiences. Our Local History Museum is open to the public on the second and fourth Sundays from April through October, and throughout the year we host a variety of special events, open houses, and fundraisers that bring people together while celebrating Cridersville’s past. From school tours and youth programs to seasonal events at the Bowsher Log Home and museum, we invite you to explore, learn, and enjoy the stories that make our community unique.

Museum Hours: Second and Fourth Sunday of each month (April–October), 1:00–4:00 p.m.

Who We Are

The Cridersville Historical Society was established in 1987 when the Bowsher Family donated the fully restored Daniel and Catherine Bowsher Log Home on East Main Street. The society soon realized it needed more space to tell the many stories of Cridersville’s past. In 1990, it purchased the former EUB Church on W. Sugar Street. Over the years, it has been given many remarkable artifacts, photographs, and documents that tell the stories of Cridersville’s history in our Timeline Gallery.

Founded: 1987 • Nonprofit: 501(c)(3)
