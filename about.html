Our Mission

Preserving Cridersville’s History for Future Generations

Our Mission is to save our local heritage for future generations. We do this through exhibits at our museums, our special public education programs, and our activities with local students.

Our Story

The Cridersville Historical Society was established in 1987 when the Bowsher Family donated the fully restored Daniel and Catherine Bowsher Log Home on East Main Street. The society soon realized it needed more space to tell the many stories of Cridersville’s past. In 1990, it purchased the former EUB Church on W. Sugar Street. Over the years, it has been given many remarkable artifacts, photographs, and documents that tell the stories of Cridersville’s history in our Timeline Gallery.

What We Do

  • Operate the Cridersville Local History Museum (open to the public the second and fourth Sunday of each month from April through October).
  • Preserve and display artifacts, photographs, and records related to local history.
  • Maintain and interpret the Bowsher Log Home.
  • Provide educational tours for local elementary students.
  • Offer youth programming through the Young Historians Club.
  • Host community events including: Beef and Noodle Dinner, Soup Supper, Quarter Auction, Fireman’s Jamboree open houses, Halloween at the Museum, Tree Lighting at the Bowsher Log Home, Christmas on Main Street.
  • Organize fundraisers to support preservation and educational efforts.

Volunteer

Our members volunteer at events and serve as museum tour guides. We provide a script and training for those interested in being a guide.
