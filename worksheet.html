Brief Origin Story:
The Cridersville Historical Society was established in 1987 when the Bowsher Family
donated the fully restored Daniel and Catherine Bowsher Log Home on East Main Street.
The society soon realized it needed more space to tell the many stories of Cridersville’s past.
In 1990, it purchased the former EUB Church on W. Sugar Street. Over the years, it has been
given many remarkable artifacts, photographs, and documents that tell the stories of
Cridersville’s history in our Timeline Gallery.
One-sentence Mission Statement:
Preserving Cridersville’s History for Future Generations
Expanded Mission Statement:
Our Mission is to save our local heritage for future generations. We do this through exhibits
at our museums, our special public education programs, and our activities with local
students.

5. Who We Are
What does the Society do?
The Cridersville Historical Society works year-round to preserve local history while creating
meaningful opportunities for the community to learn, gather, and connect through shared
heritage.
● Operate the Cridersville Local History Museum, open to the public on the second and
fourth Sunday of each month from April through October

● Preserve and display artifacts, photographs, and records related to local history
● Maintain and interpret the Bowsher Log Home
● Provide educational tours for local elementary students
● Offer youth programming through the Young Historians Club
● Host community events, including:
○ Beef and Noodle Dinner
○ Soup Supper
○ Quarter Auction
○ Fireman’s Jamboree open houses
○ Halloween at the Museum
○ Tree Lighting at the Bowsher Log Home
○ Christmas on Main Street
● Organize fundraisers to support preservation and educational efforts
Volunteer opportunities:
Our members volunteer at events and be tour guides at the museum. We provide script and
training for those interested in being a guide.

6. Where We Are (repeat per location)
Location Name:

Address:
111 W. Sugar Street, Cridersville, Ohio 45806
Hours / Access:
Second and Fourth Sunday of each month April through October
1:00-4:00 p.m.
Parking info:
Street parking and behind museum parking
Description:
The Cridersville Local History Museum has grown into a space filled with stories, exhibits,
and educational experiences that are actively shared with the community. Our current

museum layout features themed exhibit areas that guide visitors through Cridersville’s
history, from early settlement and industry to everyday life and community traditions. The
museum regularly welcomes visitors of all ages, hosts school tours and youth groups, and
relies on trained volunteers to help interpret exhibits and share local stories.
Location Name:
Bowsher Log Home
Address:
East Main Street Cridersville
Parking info:
Parking at Cridersville Health Care Center 603 E. Main Street
Description:
The Bowsher Log Cabin is a restored 19th-century log home that reflects the early
settlement and craftsmanship of the Cridersville area. Donated by the Bowsher family, the
cabin now serves as a historic landmark and educational space, offering visitors a glimpse
into daily life during the village’s early years and providing a tangible connection to
Cridersville’s pioneer past.
